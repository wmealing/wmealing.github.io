Erlang Remote Shell for Rapid Development - Nov 2025

Tired of the compile-restart-cycle anti-pattern in Erlang development? It sacrifices the corye advantage of OTP: continuous introspection. Using the remote shell workflow is the answer, transforming your text editor, specifically Emacs, into a dynamic window directly into your running Erlang system.

Stop treating your Erlang application like a fragile, traditional compiled binary. By leveraging the oldschool power of remsh (remote shell), you gain the ability to inspect, interact with, and even update code in-situ. This is the essence of Erlang's "operational elegance"—it's not just "let it crash," it's "fix it in a live system." Embrace this workflow for true live, continuous development and maintenance, allowing you to build the highly available, fault-tolerant systems Erlang was designed for, without ever needing to stop.
